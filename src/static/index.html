Revenue Cycle Management Courses for Saudi Healthcare

Comprehensive Two-Level Training Program Designed Specifically for the Saudi Market

Level 1: Beginner

Target Audience:

Fresh graduates, new entrants to healthcare administration, and individuals seeking foundational knowledge in RCM within the Saudi Arabian healthcare context.

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand the fundamental concepts and importance of Revenue Cycle Management in healthcare
  • Identify the key stages of the RCM process
  • Gain an overview of the Saudi Arabian healthcare system and its regulatory bodies
  • Comprehend basic health insurance principles and their role in RCM in KSA
  • Recognize the significance of accurate patient registration and pre-authorization
  • Understand the basics of claims submission and payment posting
  • Identify common challenges in RCM and their basic solutions

Module 1: Introduction to Healthcare RCM (2 sessions, 3 hours each)

Session 1: RCM Fundamentals
  • Definition and importance of RCM in healthcare
  • Overview of the RCM cycle: from patient admission to final payment
  • Key stakeholders in the RCM process (providers, payers, patients)
Session 2: Saudi Healthcare Landscape
  • Structure of the Saudi healthcare system (MOH, private sector)
  • Introduction to key regulatory bodies: MOH, CHI, CBAHI
  • Vision 2030 and its impact on healthcare

Module 2: Patient Access and Financial Clearance (3 sessions, 3 hours each)

Session 1: Patient Registration and Demographics
  • Importance of accurate patient data collection
  • Key information required for RCM
  • Data integrity and its impact on the revenue cycle
Session 2: Eligibility and Benefits Verification
  • Understanding health insurance in KSA
  • Process of verifying patient eligibility and insurance benefits
  • Common challenges in eligibility verification
Session 3: Pre-authorization and Referrals
  • Role of pre-authorization in RCM
  • Process for obtaining pre-authorization for services
  • Managing referrals and their financial implications

Module 3: Basic Medical Billing and Claims (3 sessions, 3 hours each)

Session 1: Introduction to Medical Billing
  • Basic concepts of medical billing
  • Types of claims and billing forms in KSA (overview)
  • Introduction to the Saudi Billing System (SBS)
Session 2: Claims Submission
  • Process of submitting clean claims
  • Common reasons for claim rejections and denials
  • Introduction to electronic claims submission
Session 3: Payment Posting and Reconciliation
  • Understanding payment posting
  • Basic reconciliation of payments with claims
  • Introduction to Explanation of Benefits (EOB) and Electronic Remittance Advice (ERA)

Module 4: RCM Compliance and Ethics (1 session, 3 hours)

Session 1: Introduction to Compliance
  • Importance of compliance in RCM
  • Overview of ethical considerations in billing and coding
  • Data security and patient privacy (basic concepts)
Total Duration: 9 sessions, 27 hours

Level 2: Advanced

Target Audience:

Experienced RCM professionals, medical coders, billers, financial analysts, and healthcare administrators seeking in-depth knowledge and practical skills in advanced RCM processes specific to the Saudi Arabian healthcare market.

Learning Objectives:

  • Master advanced RCM processes, including complex claims management and denial resolution
  • Apply detailed knowledge of Saudi medical coding standards (ICD-10-AM, ACHI, SBS)
  • Understand and implement effective pricing strategies within the Saudi healthcare regulatory framework
  • Analyze and optimize RCM performance using key metrics and financial reporting
  • Navigate and ensure compliance with the latest Saudi healthcare regulations and insurance protocols
  • Develop strategies for effective RCM ecosystem management, including technology integration

Module 1: Advanced Patient Access and Financial Management (3 sessions, 3 hours each)

Session 1: Advanced Eligibility and Benefits Verification
  • Complex insurance plans and their impact on RCM in KSA
  • Handling challenging eligibility scenarios and exceptions
  • Deep dive into CHI regulations for eligibility
Session 2: Comprehensive Pre-authorization and Service Authorization
  • Managing high-cost procedures and specialized services pre-authorization
  • Strategies for reducing pre-authorization denials
  • Integration of pre-authorization with clinical pathways
Session 3: Patient Financial Counseling and Collections
  • Advanced patient financial communication techniques
  • Effective strategies for patient collections and payment plans
  • Legal and ethical considerations in collections within KSA

Module 2: In-depth Medical Coding and Documentation (4 sessions, 3 hours each)

Session 1: ICD-10-AM and ACHI in Detail
  • Advanced application of ICD-10-AM for diagnosis coding
  • Detailed understanding of ACHI for procedure coding
  • Case studies and practical exercises for complex coding scenarios
Session 2: Saudi Billing System (SBS) Mastery
  • Comprehensive review of SBS structure and components
  • Mapping provider services to standard SBS code sets
  • Latest updates and changes in SBS V2 and SBSCS
Session 3: Clinical Documentation Improvement (CDI) for RCM
  • Importance of accurate and complete clinical documentation
  • Strategies for improving documentation to support coding and billing
  • Impact of CDI on DRG assignment and reimbursement
Session 4: Coding Audits and Quality Assurance
  • Conducting internal coding audits
  • Identifying coding errors and compliance risks
  • Implementing quality assurance programs for coding accuracy

Module 3: Healthcare Pricing and Reimbursement Strategies (3 sessions, 3 hours each)

Session 1: Principles of Healthcare Pricing in KSA
  • Factors influencing healthcare pricing in the Saudi market
  • Understanding different pricing models (e.g., fee-for-service, bundled payments)
  • Impact of regulatory bodies on pricing
Session 2: Reimbursement Methodologies and Payer Contracts
  • Detailed analysis of various reimbursement methodologies in KSA
  • Negotiating and managing payer contracts
  • Understanding contract terms and their financial implications
Session 3: Revenue Integrity and Charge Description Master (CDM) Management
  • Ensuring revenue integrity through accurate charging
  • Developing and maintaining an effective CDM
  • Strategies for charge capture optimization

Module 4: Advanced Claims Management and Denial Resolution (3 sessions, 3 hours each)

Session 1: Advanced Claims Submission and EDI
  • Optimizing electronic data interchange (EDI) for claims
  • Troubleshooting complex claims submission issues
  • Integration with NPHIES (National Platform for Health Information Exchange Services)
Session 2: Denial Management and Appeals
  • In-depth analysis of denial reasons and categories
  • Developing effective denial prevention strategies
  • Managing the appeals process for denied claims
Session 3: Accounts Receivable Management and Collections
  • Advanced AR follow-up techniques
  • Strategies for managing aged accounts and bad debt
  • Performance metrics for AR and collections

Module 5: RCM Analytics, Technology, and Future Trends (2 sessions, 3 hours each)

Session 1: RCM Performance Analytics and KPIs
  • Identifying and tracking key performance indicators (KPIs) for RCM
  • Using data analytics to identify trends and areas for improvement
  • Financial reporting and dashboard creation
Session 2: RCM Technology and Future Outlook
  • Overview of RCM software and automation tools
  • Impact of AI and machine learning on RCM
  • Future trends and challenges in Saudi healthcare RCM
Total Duration: 15 sessions, 45 hours
